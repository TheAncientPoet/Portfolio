My Name is

David Donahue

And I am a 24 year old native of East Falmouth, Massachusetts.

I graduated from Falmouth High School in 2009 and in the following autumn, I started at Fitchburg State University to pursue a Major in Film & Video.

By my second semester, I began taking a series of Graphic Design courses that allowed me to declare a dual-concentration within the Communications Major.

I still continued with Film & Video. And I continued furthering my studies, branching into other fields like Web Design and Art Design.

When the Fall semester of 2012 rolled around, I was offered a spot in the new Game Design Workshop course, in which I excelled. The following semester I took part in two more Game Design courses, and with the new offering of a Game Design Major for students of the University, I eagerly became a Double Major with Three Concentrations.


After eleven semesters with Fitchburg State, I present to you my Portfolio Website. Here you will find a collection of my best works from the last six years. I do hope you find them enjoyable.

Colonists of the Spiral Nebula: First Edition is the pilot entry to a Franchise that at its core is about Space Ships and Asteroids. Aimed at mobile devices, Colonists is an illustrated attempt to recreate the look and feel of classic arcade shooters.

The game received early popularity during its birth month of October 2012, and through the many months of development since, has gone on into merchandising, publicity, a mini-game, and a sequel currently in beta development.

I have personally acted as the game's lead designer, artist, and mathematician. From my work with Colonists, I have been able to see the world and attack problem-solving in entirely new ways.

Circles is an experiment with Mathematics. It is not a game, however the research that has gone into developing the project is substantial enough for its inclusion.

It is aptly named, as the orbiting spheres represent the results of a two-year query into "How to Make an Object Move in a Circle".

The movements are entirely mathematical, and it was my personal quest to figure out how to make it all work. And the final work goes beyond circles, also encompassing ellipsoids, spirals, and corkscrews.

These mathematical feats are important because they help pave the way for development of Artificial Intelligence in Game Design, specifically in designing paths for the entities to travel on. Further research is currently being worked on to have such A.I. travel along the paths of a Figure 8 and even polygonal pathways.
